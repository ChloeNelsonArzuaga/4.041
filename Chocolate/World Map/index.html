Cacao Production Areas

There are three main types of of cacao pods that are grown around the world. The most common pod type and the type that forms the magority of bulk blends is Forestero. The Criollo plant is the most rare due to its suseptibility to disease. The Trinitario plant is a hybrid between the Forestero and Criollo plants, giving it some of the benifits of the Criollo plant without the fragility. The last type of pod is the National plant. This pod is only grown in Ecuador and is often genetically mixed with at least a small portion of the more common plants. Since the Forestero is by far the most popular pod, the Cirollo, Trinitario, and national varieties are used more sparinly and delibrarly based on their specific flavor contributions.
